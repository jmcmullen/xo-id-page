Introducing XO-id

Teacher Student Admin

Teacher

Step 1.

When you first sign-up you’ll need to enter in something called a “school secret” as an added layer of security.

People who don’t know the secret won’t be able to sign-up for an XO-id. The secret is set by your One Education administrator – potentially the principal, deputy, or ICT coordinator. To keep your secrets safe, we can’t tell you what it is… but we can tell you who to talk to! If you get stuck, give us a call.

Step 2.

Once signed up, a teacher can go to their profile and change their name or password at any time.

If you’re on an XO, you can also press the green edit button to create a cute little avatar. Make it look like you, so that your students have an easier time recognising you!

Step 3.

Once you’re all set and signed in, it’s time to create a class!

Press the green plus in the bottom right, type in a name and select a grade. You can select multiple grades if you have a composite class.

It’s as easy as that!

Step 4.

Now it’s time to add students to your class.

Click the green plus.

Fill in the name, gender and indigenous status.

Once a student has been added, you’ll be able to search for them in future.



Step 5.

Before your students are able to sign-in to their XOs, you will have to unlock your classroom.

This is another security feature that keeps student information safe.

Once unlocked, students have a 20 minute window to request a sign-in on their XO. If they miss the window then, you can always unlock it again.

Student

Step 1.

First things first, let’s make sure the XO knows what school it’s at. This should automatically display your school’s name – but if it doesn’t, then press “Change school” and find your school by name or post code.

Without the right school, the XO won’t know which teachers to display!

Step 2.

Then the student finds their teacher.

This is why it’s important to make an accurate avatar that is easy for kids to recognise as you. It’s especially helpful for younger students who are still learning to read.

Step 3.

Once the teacher has unlocked the classroom, the students can see a list of their names (and avatars, if they have made them previously.)

They simply select their own name, and then ask to sign in to their device.

Step 4.

Every XO will display a unique robot generated from the device serial number when a student signs in. That same robot will appear in the teacher’s class list, next to the student’s name.

The teacher simply verifies that the two robots match – and clicks the orange button. When it changes to green, the student is verified and logged into their device! No need to remember difficult passwords!

Admin


As an administrator, the security of the school is in your hands.

It’s your job to verify teacher email addresses, and ensure that there are no impostors or cheeky kids with access to student data.

…to help with this, we’re also introducing the school secret.

When you first sign up, you will choose the secret password for your school. Only teachers who know the secret will be able to sign up and join your school. Choose something fabulous!
